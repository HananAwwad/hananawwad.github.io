Hi. I'm Hanan Awwad.

Software engineer who loves solving real-world problems.

I am a computer engineering graduate student specializing in cloud-native and serverless computing systems. I am currently a graduate research assistant at the Cirrus Lab, where I work under the supervision of Prof. Mohammad Shahrad. My research focuses on the design of scalable and efficient cloud infrastructure, with particular interests in serverless systems, observability, and sustainability-aware computing.
In parallel with my academic research, I have worked at Solution Scholar under the supervision of Prof. Ivan Beschastnikh, where I developed efficient, adaptive energy observability for cloud microservices, using distributed tracing and smart sampling to precisely attribute per-request energy usage with minimal runtime and energy overhead.
Before my graduate studies, I worked on large-scale cloud-based services at IQVIA, the world’s largest healthcare data and analytics company, where I developed strong practical expertise in software engineering and cloud-native technologies.
I am currently seeking internship opportunities in software engineering, cloud engineering, or DevOps, where I can apply both my research background and hands-on experience to real-world systems.
